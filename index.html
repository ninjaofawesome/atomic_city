Richland:

An Atomic City

In 1943, the sleepy agricultural community of Richland, Washington changed seemingly overnight to a town of around 300 people to a town with thousands of residents. Waves of people migrated northwestward in search of stable work for outstanding pay for the newly minted Hanford Engineering Works, and they all needed a place to live.. Fast.

Local historian and author, Richard Nordgren, examines how the government handled this rapid population boom in his interactive, highly detailed new book, Richland: An Atomic City. Nordgren entices the readers into exploring the area with a rich history of the town prior to its wartime effort, as well as a detailed account of how G. Albin Pehrson and the government worked together to quickly design and build an entire city in the matter of just two short years. The second half of the book leads readers through seven distinct walks through town, featuring unique residences as well as predominant architectural trends in that area.

Take A Walk Around Town

Each walking tour features a distinct section of town, allowing for readers to stop and admire many unique residences and housing types. The walks are set at a comfortable pace and distance, great for everyone to learn about this great city.

Take a peek at all the places you'll go on each tour below.

  • The Southern District

    Walk the diverse urban landscape from Cullum Avenue to Gillespie Streets. Don't miss the Mystery Door when taking in all the sights here!

  • The Gold Coast

    Relish one of Richland's most opulent areas, located between Hains Street and George Washington Way. The rarest jewels of G.Albin Pehrsons work are located here, its a must see.

  • The North District

    Discover the hub of Richland in the North district. Located between George Washington Way and McMurray Street, there's rich history on every corner and a vibrant, eclectic business district today.

  • The Central District

    Between George Washington Way and Williams Street you'll find many of the historical pinpoints that welcomed visitors and kept Richland running smoothly.

  • The Northwestern District

    Traversing the area between Swift Street and Wright, you'll encounter riverside views, final resting places and a rare converted house.

  • The West District

    The houses here are cute as a button! Visit Richland's postwar housing area featuring predominantly smaller homes and ranch houses.

  • The Southwest District

    From Lee Street to Duportail Road, this area of Richland has a lot to offer. Home to the Richland High Bombers, Carmichael Middle School and a variety of community centers, there's photo opportunities aplenty.
