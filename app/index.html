Richland:

An Atomic City

Founded in 19XX, this tiny hamlet sitting on the banks of the Columbia River in Eastern Washington is a town rich in history. Richland played a seminal, destructive role aiding to the conclusion of the Second World War, whilst planting the seeds for a town rich in Science, Technology, Research and Engineering.

In the course of just a few short years, a farm town that was once scattering like dust in a windstorm boasted over 20,000 residents, thanks to the Manhattan Project. How did the city rise to the demand of housing its new residents, in wartime and beyond?

Author Richard Nordgren explores the government's role in homebuilding from 1943 through the end of the cold war in this book. Covering all areas of town, Richland: An Atomic City serves as a walking tour companion and in-depth history of the town he calls home.
