Richland:

An Atomic City

Founded in 19XX, this tiny hamlet sitting on the banks of the Columbia River in Eastern Washington is a town rich in history. Richland played a crucial role aiding to the conclusion of the Second World War, whilst planting the seeds for a thriving urban hub rich in Science, Technology, Research and Engineering.

In the course of just a few short years, a farm town that was once scattering like dust in a windstorm boasted over 20,000 residents, thanks to the Manhattan Project. How did the city rise to the demand of housing its new residents, in wartime and beyond?
